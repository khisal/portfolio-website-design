Development Engineer · Project Manager · Maritime Autonomy

I build AI systems that operate in the real world, not just in simulation. My work sits at the intersection of computer vision, sensor fusion, robotics and explainable AI, with a focus on safety-critical autonomous applications.

Currently, at marinom I lead the end-to-end technical development and project management of a BMFTR-funded collaborative research project with academic and industrial partners. I am building a real-time situational awareness and navigation assistance system from the ground up, covering multi-sensor fusion, regulation-compliant decision support, and explainable AI for safety-critical decision logging. Alongside the technical work, I handle consortium coordination, milestone reporting, and BMFTR deliverables across multiple work packages.

Earlier in my career, I worked in aerial robotics on multi-target detection with RGB/thermal imaging on Nvidia Jetson and ROS, and contributed to academic research across Brain-Computer Interfaces, agricultural imaging, and synthetic dataset generation.

My background spans about ten years of combined academic and industry experience in Germany, with a BS in Electrical Engineering and an MSc in Control, Microsystems and Microelectronics.
